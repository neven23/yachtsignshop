Tips for Choosing the Right Boat Name

Keep it readable. Your name will be viewed from a distance, often at speed. Shorter names (under 15 characters) tend to look better on a transom and are easier for harbormasters to read over the radio.

Think about the font. Some names suit serif typefaces, others look better in clean sans-serifs. Our design tool lets you preview your name in multiple fonts before committing.

Check the registry. If you plan to document your vessel with the USCG, you'll need to verify your name is available. You can search the USCG documentation database online.

Consider the material. A name with lots of curves and serifs looks stunning in polished brass. Angular, modern names pair well with brushed stainless steel. And names you want visible at night are perfect for LED-illuminated acrylic.
